Why study difficult intubation?

Difficult and failed tracheal intubation are associated with hypoxia, hemodynamic instability, and peri‑operative morbidity. Multiple bedside scores (Mallampati, thyromental distance, STOP‑BANG, Al‑Ganzouri, etc.) have been proposed to anticipate a challenging airway, with heterogeneous performance across settings.

intuB is a prototype web application that allows clinicians to log intubation episodes in a structured way and to explore how simple models perform on their own data. It is intended for education and quality improvement projects, not for real‑time triage.

This tool does not replace clinical judgement, guidelines, or local airway management protocols.
